Top Safari Destinations

Uganda's Wild Wonders

UNESCO World Heritage

Bwindi Impenetrable Forest

Home to nearly half of the world's remaining mountain gorillas, Bwindi is a primeval rainforest draped in mist and mystery. A gorilla trekking permit grants you one precious hour with a habituated gorilla family — an experience that changes how you see the world.

Best: Jan–Feb, Jun–Sep SW Uganda

Uganda's Largest Park

Murchison Falls National Park

Where the mighty Nile squeezes through a 7-metre gorge with thunderous force — Murchison Falls is one of Africa's most dramatic spectacles. Beyond the falls, the park delivers classic game drives featuring elephants, lions, hippos, and Rothschild's giraffes.

Best: Dec–Feb, Jun–Sep NW Uganda
Book This Trip
Big Five Territory

Queen Elizabeth National Park

Famous for its tree-climbing lions in the Ishasha sector, Queen Elizabeth NP also dazzles with hippo-laden channels, enormous elephant herds, and over 600 bird species. A boat cruise along the Kazinga Channel is one of Uganda's most unforgettable experiences.

Best: Jun–Sep, Dec–Feb W Uganda
Book This Trip
Mountains of the Moon

Rwenzori Mountains

Africa's third-highest mountain range and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Rwenzoris offer some of the continent's most challenging and rewarding mountain trekking. Snow-capped peaks, giant lobelias, and ancient glaciers await those who make the climb.

Best: Jan–Feb, Jun–Aug W Uganda
Book This Trip
Eastern Uganda

Sipi Falls & Mt. Elgon

A series of three stunning waterfalls cascading down the slopes of Mount Elgon, Sipi is a paradise for hikers and coffee lovers alike. Trek through Arabica coffee farms, abseiled the falls, and gaze over the plains of Eastern Uganda from one of Africa's hidden gems.

Best: Year-round E Uganda
Book This Trip
Historic Landmark

Jinja – Source of the Nile

Stand at the point where the world's longest river begins its 6,695 km journey to the Mediterranean. Jinja also offers white-water rafting, bungee jumping over the Nile, kayaking, and quad biking — making it Uganda's adventure capital.

Best: Year-round SE Uganda
